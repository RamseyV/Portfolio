Bachelors of Science in Computer Science

Computer Science Classes

  • CSC 141 - Comp Sci I

    An introduction to programming using Java.

  • CSC 142 - Comp Sci II

    An introduction of classes, objects, arrays, sorting, searching, reccursion, and graphical user interfaces (GUIs).

  • CSC 240 - Comp Sci III

    This course focuses on more advanced topics in object-oriented programming, including project design,planning, and testing using milestones and checklists.

  • CSC 220 - Foundations of Computer Science

    Topics include regular and context free grammars and languages, computational logic, finite state machines, and parsing.

  • CSC 241 - Data Structures & Algorithms

    Data structures and related algorithms are studied using object-oriented programming, such as C++.

  • CSC 242 - Computer Organization

    This course teaches introductory topics in computer architecture and hardware design as well as the basics of assembly language.

  • CSC 301 - Computer Security I

    An introduction to Computer Security and the ethical underpinnings of security.

  • CSC 302 - Computer Security II

    Principles and current technological developments in computer security. Topics include: security requirements, attack models, cryptography, authentication, and system security.

  • CSC 335 - Data Communications and Networking I

    An overview of the various aspects of modern data and telecommunications.

  • CSC 345 Programming Language Concepts & Paradigms

    An examination of the conceptual underpinning of programming languages and of the paradigms into which they fall.

  • CSC 402 - Software Engineering

    This course explores a variety of processes for developing software, including the PSP from the Software Engineering Institute, the SEI's CMMI, and agile processes, including eXtreme Programming and Scrum.

  • CSC 476 - Game Development

    This project-based course is concerned with game development and scripting using a modern game engine, such as Unity, with a modern programming language, such as C#.

  • CSC 481 - Artificial Intellignece

    Topics will be drawn from any of those comprising the field of AI such as agent architectures, automatic truth maintenance, constraint satisfaction, expert systems, fuzzy logic, games, genetic algorithms, knowledge representation, machine learning, neural networks and connectionism, natural language processing, planning, reasoning, robotics, search, theorem proving, and vision.

    • Math Classes

  • MAT 121 - Statistics

    Frequency distributions, measures of central tendency and variability, probability and theoretical distribution, significance of differences, and hypothesis testing.

  • MAT 151 - Deiscrete Mathematics

    Set theory, Boolean logic, elementary combinatorics, proofs, simple graph theory, and simple probability.

  • MAT 161 - Calculus I

    Differential and integral calculus of real-valued functions of a single real variable, with applications.

  • MAT 162 - Calculus II

    Continuation of MAT 161 including the study of series, methods of integration, transcendental functions, and applications to the sciences.

  • MAT 261 - Calculus III

    The calculus of several variables. Topics include polar coordinates, vectors and three-dimensional analytic geometry, differentiation of functions of several variables, multiple integrals, and line and surface integrals.
